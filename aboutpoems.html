Poems are literary works that express feelings, thoughts, or ideas in a rhythmic and often figurative language.

10 of the Best Australian Poems Everyone Should Read:

  • Banjo Paterson, ‘The Man from Snowy River’
  • Ada Cambridge, ‘After Our Likeness’
  • Henry Lawson, ‘Up the Country’
  • Dorothea Mackellar, ‘My Country’
  • Gwen Harwood, ‘In the Park’
  • A. D. Hope, ‘Australia’
  • Les Murray, ‘An Absolutely Ordinary Rainbow’
  • Oodgeroo Noonuccal, ‘We Are Going’
  • Judith Wright, ‘Eve to Her Daughters’
  • Banjo Paterson and Marie Cowan, ‘Waltzing Matilda’

    Reading poetry has many benefits, including:

    • Inspiring readers to reflect, dream, reminisce, observe, and fantasize.
    • Encouraging a certain interconnectedness and helping establish a sense of community between oneself and others.
    • Improving vocabulary and verbal dexterity.
    • Providing comfort and boosting mood during periods of stress, trauma and grief.
    • Motivating children to read, building phonemic awareness and building essential skills like vocabulary, fluency, expression, and writing.
